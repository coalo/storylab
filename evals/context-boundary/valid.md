## Reader-facing promise

An intimate contest between livelihood, pride, and reluctant trust.

## POV boundary

Lin Yue knows the shop is short of rice but does not know who bought the last cart.

## Immediate lived state

She is cold, hungry, and trying to open before the market bell.

## Immediate desire

She wants enough stock to serve the noon crowd without borrowing.

## Counterforce desire

The mill owner wants payment for an older debt before releasing grain.

## Dramatic pressure

Waiting loses the market; accepting his terms exposes a private weakness.

## Canon facts

- The shop has twelve copper coins.

## Required state change

By the end, the grain problem and their balance of leverage must both be different.

## Protected information

- The identity of the earlier buyer remains unknown.

## Creative freedom

The author owns action, dialogue, rhythm, imagery, and the way change occurs.
