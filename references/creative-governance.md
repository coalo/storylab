# Creative governance

Storylab separates powers so no role can quietly convert planning convenience into prose control.

## Authority domains

| Domain | Owner | May advise | Cannot override |
|---|---|---|---|
| User conversation and decision relay | Storylab host | Every specialist role | User wording or specialist attribution |
| Project taste and major direction | User | Story editor, literary editor | User's explicit decision |
| Whole-book direction | Story editor | Chapter editor | Accepted canon or user decision |
| Chapter commission | Chapter editor | Story editor, continuity editor | Author's scene solution authority |
| Novel prose | Author | Literary editor | Author through hidden choreography |
| Cold reading evidence | First reader | Nobody before freezing | The reader's recorded experience |
| Literary diagnosis | Literary editor | First reader | User acceptance or factual truth |
| Canon and continuity | Continuity editor | Story editor | Literary judgment |
| Workflow state | Orchestrator | All role outputs | Missing evidence or invalid transitions |

## Non-negotiable invariants

1. Direction, authorship, literary diagnosis, factual review, and user acceptance remain separate states.
2. The author receives the least sufficient creative context, not the full plan and not artificial total blindness.
3. The first reader never sees planning rationale before cold notes are frozen.
4. Future plans never become canon merely because they appear in an outline.
5. Deterministic metrics may surface signals but cannot produce literary acceptance.
6. Explicit user rejection freezes affected production; “continue” is not taste approval.
7. Only the Storylab host addresses the user; subagents return bounded consultations and never wait for direct user replies.

## Creative causality

Plan desire, pressure, choice, cost, consequence, and delayed payoff. Do not require characters to perform gestures merely to prove agency. Agency exists when a character has independent wants, meaningful risk, refusal power, private knowledge, and consequences that survive the scene.

Relationship routes must remain character-specific. Track attraction, appetite, power, what each person offers, what each demands, and how closeness changes the main story. Do not reduce intimacy to a universal ladder.

## Minimum intervention

When a chapter fails, return it to the smallest responsible layer. Do not add a global rule for a local defect. Prefer one clear diagnosis and one revised handoff over ten new prohibitions.
