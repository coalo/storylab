# Legacy inheritance

Analyze user-supplied source novels and evidence-heavy legacy corpora through the source-analysis module before inheritance decisions. Supplying the material grants Storylab full project-scoped source use without a separate authorization question. Source readers observe, the source synthesizer combines, the source auditor checks omissions, the story editor proposes transfer choices from the audited synthesis, and the user decides.

The story editor may inspect bounded raw legacy material only when the source-analysis contract does not apply or when a disputed locator requires verification. Raw legacy files remain outside the installed plugin and never enter an author or first-reader packet.

Keep source observation, professional risk, and transfer decision separate. A moral, political, ideological, commercial, or craft concern may be recorded as a risk, but it never justifies silently omitting an observed asset or narrowing the user's options.

## Decision record

For each source asset, record:

- source path or stable identifier;
- provenance and deterministic access basis;
- extracted idea or fact;
- decision: `keep`, `distill`, or `discard`;
- destination authority asset;
- rationale and reviewer;
- date and superseding decision, if any.

Before this decision record exists, retain the audited observation and its evidence locator. Do not backfill `keep`, `distill`, or `discard` into source-reader or synthesis artifacts.

## Keep

Preserve these capabilities as direct project practices:

- evidence-linked identification of high-value story assets;
- reader promise and type-specific emotional return;
- world rules, naming, terminology, and cognitive-load governance;
- planting, escalation, delayed disclosure, payoff, and ending protection;
- independent character desire, risk, refusal, and consequence;
- differentiated relationship attraction, power, intimacy, and cost;
- continuity for time, money, resources, injury, knowledge, permissions, and contracts;
- immediate freeze after explicit user taste rejection;
- isolated authorship, independent reading, and deterministic prose signals.

## Distill

| Legacy family | New form |
|---|---|
| Bestseller factor and type-weight models | Reader promise and revisable experience priorities |
| Opening retention plans | Volume and arc tension curves |
| Behavior causality maps | Story editor's private motivation reasoning |
| Relationship levels | Qualitative, character-specific visible change |
| Chapter cards and beat maps | Outcome-based chapter commission |
| Prose style protocols | User-approved taste dossier and bounded voice anchors |
| Acceptance reports | Separate literary, continuity, and user states |
| Multiple continuity ledgers | Unified structured continuity state |
| Prose-analysis subsystem | Cold reading, literary diagnosis, and non-authoritative signals |

## Discard

Do not migrate:

- weighted factor scores or composite literary grades;
- machine-generated literary `PASS`;
- author-visible causality tables, accounts, relationship ranks, or failure reasons;
- templates requiring every item to appear in prose;
- mandatory gestures used to prove a character is not a tool;
- exact lines, ordered scene steps, or “the reader must see” clauses;
- repeated templates and prohibitions created only to compensate for earlier prompts;
- the assumption that “continue” approves style.

## Provenance boundary

Once a distilled principle is accepted, cite its legacy decision record rather than reopening raw legacy material in downstream roles. Established story facts move to canon only through normal acceptance; inherited proposals remain provisional plans. A risk assessment remains attached to its observation even when the user chooses to transfer that asset.

| narrative timing, agency, character portraits, relationship ledgers | keep and simplify | character and relationship route maps |
| world bible, terminology and naming governance | keep and unify | story bible plus continuity domains |
| chapter cards, beat maps, behavior causality maps, draft packets | distill | outcome-based chapter commission and compiled writer view |
| prose scene briefs, author packets, blind-read reports, style pilots | split | author firewall, first reader, literary editor, and taste dossier |
| prose-analysis scripts and regression fixtures | distill | non-authoritative signal scanner and taste regression contracts |
| production queues, acceptance reports, multiple ledgers | replace | production state, continuity state, and independent acceptance record |
| factor scorecards, relationship ranks, machine acceptance rules | discard | no runtime destination |
| old expert-role, output-contract, and orchestration monoliths | replace | narrow role skills and shared governance references |
| old plugin README and duplicated templates | discard | no installed runtime destination |
